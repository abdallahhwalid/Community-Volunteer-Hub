Safety Guidelines

Best practices for a secure community experience

Keeping our platform safe is a team effort. Because Community Help Hub connects people for in-person tasks, it is vital that you prioritize your physical and digital security. Please thoroughly read and follow these guidelines.

1. Meet in Public First

For tasks that do not specifically require home access (such as tutoring, returning a lost item, or tech advice), always arrange to meet in a well-lit, busy public space. Coffee shops, libraries, and community centers are excellent choices. If possible, arrange your first meeting during daylight hours.

2. Keep Communication on the Platform

Avoid sharing your personal phone number or email address right away. Use our built-in messaging system to discuss the details of the request. Scammers often try to move conversations off-platform immediately so their behavior cannot be tracked.

3. Protect Your Private Information

Never share sensitive personal details such as your Social Security Number, bank account information, passwords, or exact home address unless it is absolutely necessary for the task (like a plumbing repair) and you have built trust with the volunteer.

4. Tell a Friend or Family Member

Before you meet someone from the platform, tell a trusted friend or family member exactly where you are going, who you are meeting (share their username/profile), and what time you expect to be finished. Consider sharing your live location with a friend via your smartphone.

5. Trust Your Instincts

You are never obligated to complete a task or meet with someone if you feel uncomfortable. If a user acts aggressively, makes strange requests, or simply gives you a bad feeling, cancel the meetup immediately. Your safety always comes first.

6. Reporting Suspicious Behavior

If you encounter a user who violates these guidelines or makes you feel unsafe, please report them to us immediately at support@communityhelpub.org so we can investigate. In the event of an immediate physical emergency or threat, please contact your local emergency services (like 911) before contacting our support team.
